Participant Information & Consent Form

Ethics reference number: ERGO/FPSE/24293 Version: 3 Date: 2017-05-03
Study Title: Web Mirror II: Intent vs. Action
Investigator: Dorota Filipczuk, Richard Gomer, Dr Alper T. Alan, Dr Enrico Gerding, Prof m.c. schraefel

Please read this information carefully before deciding to take part in this research. Your participation is completely voluntary.

What is the research about? This is a research project which aims to understand how we can design useful information about online tracking. We are also creating an anonymous database of browsing history for future study. The study is a part of the Meaningful Consent in Digital Economy project.

Why have I been chosen? You have been approached because you responded to our advertisement.

What will happen to me if I take part? You will be asked to install a Chrome extension on your own machine, which will be collecting your browsing history and information on use of countermeasures. After that, you will fill online surveys related to online tracking, which will take around 30 minutes. At the end of the study, you will be asked to fill another survey online. This will also take about 30 minutes.

Are there any benefits in my taking part? The study will add to current knowledge about privacy online. You will also receive a £5 Amazon gift voucher for your participation at the end of the study.

Are there any risks involved? There are no particular risks associated with your participation.

Will my data be confidential? All data collected stored confidentially, will not be shared with anyone else and will only be used in accordance with the Data Protection Act (1998).

If you would like to access your data after your participation, change it, or withdraw it, please contact one of the investigators (e-mails: dorota@ecs.soton.ac.uk, r.gomer@soton.ac.uk) or the project supervisor (e-mail: mc@ecs.soton.ac.uk) who will arrange this.

What happens if I change my mind? You may withdraw at any time and for any reason.

What happens if something goes wrong? Should you have any concern or complaint, contact us if possible (e-mail: dorota@ecs.soton.ac.uk), otherwise please contact the FPSE Office (ergopse@soton.ac.uk) or any other authoritative body such as the Research Integrity & Governance Team (rgoinfo@soton.ac.uk).

Please continue if you agree:

I have read and understood the Participant Information (version 3 dated 2017-05-03) and have had the opportunity to ask questions about the study.
I agree to take part in this study.
I understand my participation is voluntary and I may withdraw at any time and for any reason.

Data Protection

I understand that information collected during my participation in this study is will be stored on a password protected secure University server and that this information will only be used in accordance with the Data Protection Act (1998). The DPA (1998) requires data to be processed fairly and lawfully in accordance with the rights of participants and protected by appropriate security. In addition, the DPA (1998) makes provision for an appropriate authority, such as the Police, to access data held by the study.
