Tracking Countermeasures

The information below describes ways of preventing online tracking. Please read it carefully and click "Continue".

Browser Extensions

Browser extensions such as (Disconnect) or (Ghostery) can be installed in your Web browser (Chrome, Safari, Internet Explorer, Firefox etc.) They block the 'requests' (connections) that your browser makes to known tracking websites, preventing them from tracking you.

Pros
  • Fairly simple to install
  • Block most of the tracking
Cons
  • Not available for older browsers
  • Might not be installable on corporate or school equipment
  • Sometimes need to be configured properly to block tracking (e.g. by default Ghostery only reports tracking rather than blocking it)

"Third-party cookies"

Browser settings can be changed to reject "third-party cookies". This prevents storing the "cookies" that allow your computer to be identified across multiple visits to a website. You can find out how to do it here.

Pros
  • Simple to do
  • Does not require any additional software
Cons
  • Not obvious how to do without instructions
  • Can break some websites that rely on third-party services
  • It does not stop tracking that uses "fingerprinting" instead of cookies
  • May not work for websites that track you, but are also places you visit, e.g. Facebook or Twitter

Ad Blockers

"Ad Blocker" browser extensions also block a lot of tracking, because they prevent requests to many of the websites that undertake tracking. They remove adverts from Web pages that you visit and often block the requests to the advertising network itself. Some examples include AdBlock, AdBlock Plus, Ad-aware Ad Block, Ads Blocker, Goodblock.

Pros
  • Fairly easy to install
  • Also prevents you from seeing adverts, improving load speed and battery life.
Cons
  • Not available for older browsers
  • Might not be installable on corporate and school equipment
  • May not block all tracking
  • Some websites prevent access to people using ad blockers

Opt-out

Most advertising networks provide the ability for users to opt-out of tracking. Opt-outs usually set a 'cookie' in your browser that tells the advertising network not to track you or display personalised ads. You can find out more here.

Pros
  • Can be used with any Web browser
  • Should not prevent any websites from working
Cons
  • The opt-out cookies themselves are lost if you clear your cookies
  • Not all trackers provide an opt-out mechanism
  • There are hundreds of networks to opt-out from
  • In practice, opt-outs often need to be restored/reset regularly

Clearing cookies

Clearing all of the cookies in your Web browser removes the tracking cookies that are used to identify you each time you visit a website.

Pros
  • Will cause most trackers to "forget" about your computer
  • Easy to do on all Web browsers
Cons
  • Some trackers use other means to identify you, such as fingerprinting
  • Will cause you to be logged out of all websites and have to sign in again
  • Some websites might forget personalisation settings
  • Does not prevent tracking from taking place in the future

Clearing browser history

Clearing all of the history stored in your browser does not actually affect tracking - the history collected by trackers in is completely independent of the history that is stored in your browser

Pros
  • Prevents other people with access to your computer from seeing what you have looked at on the Web
Cons
  • Does not help protect against tracking
